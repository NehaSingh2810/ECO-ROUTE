Government Regulations & Environmental Laws

Understanding the legal consequences of improper waste disposal and the importance of systematic collection

Critical Environmental Alert

Every year, thousands of animals die from consuming plastic waste thrown in open areas. Marine life, street animals, and wildlife suffer from plastic ingestion, leading to painful deaths and ecosystem collapse. Your responsible waste disposal can save lives.

High Risk

Open Area Dumping

₹5,000 - ₹25,000

Throwing waste in open areas, streets, or public spaces is strictly prohibited under environmental protection laws.

  • First offense: ₹5,000 fine + warning
  • Repeat offense: ₹25,000 fine
  • Community service: 40 hours mandatory
  • Environmental restoration fee
Critical

Plastic Waste Violation

₹10,000 - ₹50,000

Improper disposal of plastic waste and single-use plastics causing environmental damage.

  • Plastic burning: ₹50,000 fine
  • River/water body dumping: ₹25,000
  • Business license suspension possible
  • Mandatory plastic waste management training
Extreme

Industrial Waste

₹1,00,000+

Unauthorized disposal of industrial and hazardous waste materials.

  • Hazardous waste: ₹5,00,000 fine
  • Water contamination: ₹10,00,000+
  • Criminal prosecution possible
  • Business closure orders
Animal Welfare

Animal Welfare Violation

₹15,000 - ₹75,000

Waste disposal causing harm to animals and wildlife, including death from plastic consumption.

  • Animal death due to waste: ₹75,000
  • Habitat destruction: ₹50,000
  • Wildlife protection act violation
  • Ecosystem damage compensation
